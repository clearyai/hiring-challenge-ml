Rule 7
All surgically invasive devices intended for short-term use are classified in
Class II, unless:
a) Are specifically intended to control, diagnose, monitor or correct
cardiac or central circulatory system dysfunctions through direct contact with these
parts of the body, in which case they are classified in Class IV;
b) They are specifically intended to be used in direct contact with the
heart, the central circulatory system or the central nervous system, in which case they
are classified in class IV;
c) Are intended to provide energy in the form of ionizing radiation, in
which case they are classified in Class III;
d) Have a biological effect or are absorbed, in whole or in large part, in
which case they are classified in Class IV;
e) They are intended to undergo a chemical transformation in the body, in
which case they belong to Class III, unless they are placed on the teeth; or
f) they are intended to administer medicines, in which case they are
